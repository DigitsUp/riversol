Campaign Proposals
01
SMS Growth · Cross-Channel
SMS Flash Exclusive Cross-Channel Opt-In
The Opportunity
73,000 customers are opted into email but not SMS — and they're all proven buyers (spent $19+). That's a massive untapped list sitting inside an existing relationship.
The Strategy
Send a single email to this segment with an "Unlock the Code" CTA. When they tap it, it opens their Messages app with a pre-filled keyword (suggested: UNLOCK). Texting it opts them in and immediately triggers a flash sale offer — 15% off.
Segment Size
~73,000
Target KPI
10% opt-in rate — ~7,000–10,000 new SMS subscribers
Platform
Klaviyo SMS + Email
Setup
Keyword + opt-in workflow — Digitsup handles
◆
Open Item Confirm discount % and offer window
Why It Works
  • Zero impact on existing SMS subscribers
  • Pairs a list-growth mechanic with a purchase moment
  • Converts passive email readers into high-intent SMS subscribers
How It Flows
The email itself is the list-growth mechanism. A customer opens the email, taps the CTA, their phone opens a pre-composed text — they send the keyword UNLOCK — they're instantly opted in and the flash offer fires automatically. No extra steps, no forms, no friction.
The keyword trigger pairs list growth with a purchase moment. The customer who just opted in is already in an active buying headspace. That's the window.
Segment criteria: opted into email, has made a purchase ≥$19, not yet opted into SMS.
02
Loyalty · Tier Progression
Tier Milestone Nudge Silver & Gold Push
The Opportunity
Customers close to the next loyalty tier are high-intent — they've already bought in. A well-timed nudge can push them over the line and build habit around loyalty participation.
The Strategy
Two small campaign sends targeting near-miss customers:
  • Bronze → Silver: customers at $300–$399 annual spend (Silver threshold: $400)
  • Silver → Gold: customers at $600–$749 annual spend (Gold threshold: $750)
Each campaign acknowledges their current status, shows exactly what the next tier unlocks, and gives a clear, low-friction reason to get there.
Why It Works
  • Recognition drives action — customers respond to being seen
  • Small segments = low risk, high learning value
  • Data from this test informs the full tier recognition flow build
The Angle
The message isn't "spend more." It's "you're almost there." Frame the gap as progress already made, not distance remaining. Show the specific dollar amount between them and the next tier — and what that tier unlocks. That specificity is what converts.

03
Product Education · Seasonal
Summer-Ready Skin SPF 50 Sunscreen Awareness
The Opportunity
58,000 customers have spent over $19 but have never viewed a sunscreen product. These are engaged, proven buyers — they just haven't been introduced to this part of the line yet.
The Strategy
A dedicated April send introducing the SPF 50 Daily Glow Mineral Sunscreen to this segment. Position it as a natural spring-to-summer system upgrade alongside their existing moisturizer routine.
The Angle
  • No white cast, no chemical filters
  • Same lab as the serum they already trust
  • April timing is natural — this isn't a forced push, it's the right moment
Segment Size
~58,000
Timing
Mid-to-late April
Platform
Klaviyo Email
Why It Works
  • No discount needed — the story and timing do the work
  • Converts single-category buyers into multi-category customers
  • Increases AOV and deepens product relationship
The Trust Signal
These customers already have a relationship with Riversol. They've tried one product, they liked it enough to buy. The intro line writes itself: "From the same lab." The existing product relationship is the credibility transfer.
April is the natural window — spring-to-summer skin prep is a real behaviour. This isn't a product push, it's a well-timed recommendation from a brand they already trust. No offer needed. The story and the timing carry it.
Segment criteria: has made a purchase ≥$19, zero sunscreen product page views in any session.
