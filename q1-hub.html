Recommended Actions & Q2 Priorities
01
Riversol+ idle credits -- email + SMS now
3,260 members with CA$19+ unspent. Send email first: "Your credits are waiting." Follow with SMS 48 hours later for non-openers. Fastest revenue available -- days, not weeks.
02
Dormant loyalists points reactivation
36,174 customers with 350+ points. Email with points-expiry urgency or double-points event. Add SMS for non-openers at Day 3. No discount needed -- points are the incentive.
03
Add SMS to sample post-delivery flow
The 44-day conversion window is the critical nurture period. An SMS at Day 15 and Day 30 anchored around the Anti-Aging Trio and Free Retinol Neck Cream offer could meaningfully lift the 1.36% trial-to-paid conversion rate.
04
One-Hit Wonder winback -- email + SMS sequence
32,280 customers. Three emails escalating: free shipping to 15% off to GWP. If no conversion after Email 3, send one SMS as the final close. Find the trigger without leading with the deepest discount.
05
Test bundle-anchored campaigns with SMS support
Anti-Aging Trio down 46-52%. Run a bundle-focused campaign (GWP or spend-and-save) and pair with an SMS send to Riversol+ members and high-intent segments. SMS for bundle moments converts faster than email alone.
06
Skin analyzer: welcome series A/B test
Benchmark offer-led welcome (promo code) vs. skin analyzer path (personalization, no promo). The analyzer is a no-discount entry point that could reduce margin erosion while improving subscriber quality.
07
Scale Riversol+ membership to grow the best-converting flow
Riversol+ Reminders has a 16.8% placed order rate -- 3x better than any other flow. Every new member added directly increases the size and revenue of this audience.
08
Build second-purchase flow for organic first-timers
8,157 customers who trusted Riversol enough to pay without a trial. Personalized to their first product -- serum buyers get moisturizer next, Trio buyers get Eye Repair Treatment.
09
Q4 2026 -- Plan a structured BFCM strategy now
November 2025 was the strongest cohort on the board -- CA$153K in first order revenue from only 12.7K customers, with a CA$10.94 first order AOV vs. CA$2-4 every other month. BFCM drives high-intent buyers who skip the trial and buy bundles at full price. Building a BFCM campaign calendar now -- not in October -- is the single highest-leverage Q4 investment.

Business Snapshot -- Key Takeaways
  • Traffic is up slightly (+3%) but conversion has nearly halved (5.8% to 3.19%), which is the single biggest driver of order and revenue decline. This points to a top-of-funnel issue -- likely fewer trial-to-paid conversions rather than a drop in site quality.
  • The four-layer revenue compression: (1) discounts increasing -- more margin given away per order; (2) new customers down, and new customers typically carry the highest AOV because they're buying a full routine or bundle for the first time; (3) the new customers who are converting are buying single products, not bundles -- AOV compression within the new cohort itself; (4) returning customers down in both volume and basket size. Shopify's overall AOV appearing up 4% is misleading -- it's a compositional effect of fewer low-AOV sample orders in the mix, not genuine spending improvement.
  • AOV is up 4% and returning customer rate is up 6%, which means the customers who are coming back are spending more per visit. Retention is working. Acquisition is not.
  • New customer volume is down 33%, which is significant for a free trial-driven model. If fewer people are entering via trial, the downstream purchase cohort shrinks.

Sample Segment -- Key Takeaways
  • The trial funnel is the single biggest lever in Riversol's business. A 35% drop in sample volume and a 33.7% drop in conversion rate compounded into a 57% reduction in paid customers from this channel.
  • The converters who do make it through are high-value. ~$176 average spend post-sample, fast repeat purchases, and strong membership attach rate. The product and the post-sample experience are working -- the issue is volume at the top of the funnel.
  • The Free Retinol Neck Cream offer and Anti-Aging Trio are the two strongest conversion levers in the post-sample journey. These should be the anchor of every sample nurture flow and campaign targeting trial takers in the 15-60 day window.
  • The 44-day conversion window means Q1 sample data is inherently incomplete. February and March sample takers will continue converting into Q2.
Section 06
Strategic Narrative & Next Steps
6.1 -- Key Observations
01
The lifecycle is not broken -- it's volume-starved
Klaviyo held 61% of Shopify revenue (flat vs. 60.15% prior year). Flow conversion rate doubled (+108.7%). Month 1 AOV for Q1 2026 cohorts is comparable to 2025. The email and automation system is performing -- it just has fewer people flowing through it. This is an acquisition problem, not a lifecycle execution problem.
02
The sample funnel is the root cause of Q1 revenue decline
Sample orders down 35.3% (112,779 to 72,943) and trial-to-paid conversion rate down 33.7% (2.05% to 1.36%) compounded into a 57% drop in paid customers from this channel. This cascaded through every metric.
03
79,000+ dormant customers represent the fastest Q2 revenue path
Across four segments -- One-Hit Wonders (32,280), Dormant Loyalists (36,174), Organic First-Timers (8,157), and Riversol+ Idle Credits (3,260) -- there are over 79,000 customers who have demonstrated real purchase intent and gone quiet. Reactivating even 5-8% of this base generates thousands of orders without touching acquisition spend.

How to Read These Numbers

  • Revenue is down 26% YoY despite sending 24% more email. More volume, less return per send -- the story is in AOV compression and two missing flow sequences.
